Campaign Clarity

Shedding light on the campaign monetary process.

Politics involves a lot of money, and sometimes, this money may be dirty, and may be used to bribe votes. I set out to try to find a link between where the money went and how the politicians voted. Currently, the Python program at the core of the app processes data from our friends at the Sunlight Foundation and Open Secrets and presents to the user which industries gave money to politicians that voted across party lines (since politics is very partisian nowadays). Eventually, I hope to make this consider other aspects, such as the demographics of their district and their past voting tendencies to try to identify a true shift in voting pattern.

  • Date: February 2015
  • Other Contributors: Siddarth Srivatsan and Spencer Hong

Quick Macro

A simple tampermonkey to provide macros on the web

Quick Macro leverages tampermonkey to allow the user to create a web hotkey. They can configure it to link "alt-1" to any string they want, perhaps making it faster to fill out some forms.

Its accessible here.

Tic Tac Ception

A multilayered, browser-based tic tac toe game.

You must win each game of mini-tic-tac-toe to win the whole game. However, your opponent controls where you make your next move. A relatively simple game, with documentation available on its github page . Hosted online at rajshrimali.com/TicTacCeption.

SWIC

StopWatch including Clock. Simple command line timer utility.

Sometimes, you just need a stopwatch while you're coding. Maybe you're trying to see how long you can focus, or maybe you've accidentally broken X11 by updating your nvidia driver on Arch Linux without updating the rest of the xorg infrastrucutre. Who knows. This gives you the power to do that.

Fitso

Lets play together.

An app to connect those who want to play sports together. You don't need to know anyone in the area to get a pickup game going with fitso. Fitso allows users to see who is nearby and match them with a mathematical algorithm that takes into account their skill level and their age.

Random Groups

Match students in class.

Teachers have a hard time pairing students randomly and fairly. They'll resort to games with names, matching them based on their position in the class roster, or by height. To solve this, I created a very basic web application that allows a teacher to input the students in her class, select some parameters (number of groups), and get the randomly generated groups! Available here.
